# RestoFlow: Strategic Pricing & Financial Forecast Guide

This guide details the revenue models, costing strategies, and growth projections used for the RestoFlow investor presentation.

## 1. Primary Revenue Models
RestoFlow operates on a **SaaS + Fintech Hybrid** model to maximize LTV.

### A. Subscription Tiers (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
*   **Essential (£199):** For single-location cafes/venues. Core POS, 5 users.
*   **Professional (£349):** For high-volume restaurants. Full Inventory + Labour suite.
*   **Enterprise (£499+):** Multi-location groups (up to 50 venues). Consolidated reporting + Advanced API access.

### B. Payment Clip (Take Rate)
By integrating a proprietary payment processor (e.g., via Stripe Connect), RestoFlow captures **0.5% to 1.0%** of total Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV).
*   *Scale Example:* 100 restaurants doing £50k/month = £5M GMV. Take rate = £25,000 to £50,000 pure profit per month.

---

## 2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs. LTV
RestoFlow's LTV is high due to high friction of switching core POS systems.
*   **Estimated LTV:** £8,000 - £12,000 over 3 years.
*   **Target CAC:** £1,200 (2-month payback period).

---

## 3. Operational Logic (Tech ROI)
RestoFlow saves restaurants money by automating high-cost human tasks:
*   **Invoice OCR:** Saves 4 hours of manager data entry per week.
*   **Demand Scheduling:** Prevents an average of 10 hours of over-staffing per week.
*   **Recipe Costing:** Flags "Menu Leakage" (items costing more than their markup) in real-time.
